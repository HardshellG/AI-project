History of AI


Artificial intelligence was founded as an academic discipline in 1955. The field of AI research was born at a workshop at Dartmouth College in 1956,[32] where the term "Artificial Intelligence" was coined by John McCarthy to distinguish the field from cybernetics and escape the influence of the cyberneticist Norbert Wiener."machines will be capable, within twenty years, of doing any work a man can do". Marvin Minsky agreed. In the early 1980s, AI research was revived by the commercial success of expert systems,[42] a form of AI program that simulated the knowledge and analytical skills of human experts.
